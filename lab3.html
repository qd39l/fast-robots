Sensors

The Artemis board now becomes Doctor Octopus!

Posted by Owen Deng on February 22, 2022

The goal of this lab is to connect the sensors to the robot, and characterize the capabilities of the sensors we are using. The robot can only move as fast as the sampling rate of the sensors, so the capabilities of the sensors basically determine the upper limit of our robot's moving speed. The robot has three sensing components: two Time-of-Flight Sensors (ToF) and an Inertial Measurement Unit (IMU).

When it comes to sensors, it's important to have access to the sensors' specifications as well as their API libraries. For the ToF sensor (VL53L1X), we have access to the manual, the datasheet datasheet, and the headerfile for the corresponding Arduino library. For the IMU (ICM 20948), we have access to the datasheet, the Arduino library.

Hooking up the sensors

All the sensors we are working with come with a breakout board. Each sensor communicates with the Artemis board via I2C protocol.
